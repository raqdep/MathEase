What is Compound Interest?

Definition

Compound Interest is interest calculated on the initial principal and the accumulated interest of previous periods. Unlike simple interest, compound interest grows exponentially because interest is earned on both the principal and previously earned interest.

Key Characteristics:
  • Exponential Growth: Money grows faster over time due to interest on interest
  • Compounding Frequency: How often interest is calculated (annually, quarterly, monthly, etc.)
  • Time Value of Money: Money today is worth more than the same amount in the future

Compound Interest

Formula: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

Interest earned on principal + previous interest

Example: ₱10,000 at 5% annually for 3 years
Year 1: ₱10,500 | Year 2: ₱11,025 | Year 3: ₱11,576

Simple Interest

Formula: A = P(1 + rt)

Interest earned only on principal

Example: ₱10,000 at 5% annually for 3 years
Year 1: ₱10,500 | Year 2: ₱11,000 | Year 3: ₱11,500

Understanding the Formula

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

A (Final Amount)

The total amount after interest is applied

Principal + Compound Interest

P (Principal)

The initial amount of money invested or borrowed

Starting amount

r (Annual Rate)

The annual interest rate as a decimal

5% = 0.05

n (Compounding Frequency)

How many times per year interest is compounded

Annually=1, Quarterly=4, Monthly=12

Formula Breakdown

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

  • A: Final amount (principal + interest)
  • P: Principal (initial amount)
  • r: Annual interest rate (as decimal)
  • n: Compounding frequency per year
  • t: Time in years
Example

P = 10,000, r = 6%, n = 4 (quarterly), t = 2 years

r/n = 0.06/4 = 0.015
nt = 4 × 2 = 8
A = 10000(1 + 0.015)^8
A = 10000(1.015)^8 = 11,264.93

Lesson 4: Present Value and Future Value

Learn about the time value of money and how to calculate present and future values.

Time Value of Money

The concept that money available today is worth more than the same amount in the future due to its potential earning capacity. This principle is fundamental to understanding compound interest.

Future Value (FV)

The value of money at a future date, given a specific interest rate and compounding frequency.

FV = PV(1 + r/n)^(nt)

Example:

What will ₱5,000 be worth in 3 years at 8% compounded quarterly?

PV = ₱5,000, r = 0.08, n = 4, t = 3
FV = 5000(1 + 0.08/4)^(4×3)
FV = 5000(1.02)^12 = ₱6,345.20

Present Value (PV)

The current value of money to be received in the future, discounted at a specific interest rate.

PV = FV / (1 + r/n)^(nt)

Example:

What is the present value of ₱10,000 to be received in 2 years at 6% compounded monthly?

FV = ₱10,000, r = 0.06, n = 12, t = 2
PV = 10000 / (1 + 0.06/12)^(12×2)
PV = 10000 / (1.005)^24 = ₱8,884.35
