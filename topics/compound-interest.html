Comprehensive Guide to Compound Interest

What is Compound Interest?

Compound Interest is interest calculated on the initial principal and the accumulated interest of previous periods. Unlike simple interest, compound interest grows exponentially because interest is earned on both the principal and previously earned interest. This creates a "snowball effect" where your money grows faster over time!

📐 The Fundamental Formula:

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

Final Amount = Principal × (1 + Rate/Compounding Frequency)^(Compounding Frequency × Time)

A = Final Amount (Principal + Interest)
P = Principal (initial amount)
r = Annual interest rate (as decimal)
n = Compounding frequency per year
t = Time in years

⚠️ Important: Always convert the percentage rate to decimal form before calculation (e.g., 5% = 0.05). The compounding frequency (n) determines how many times per year interest is added to your account.

Key Characteristics:
  • Exponential Growth: Money grows faster over time due to interest on interest. Each period, you earn interest on a larger amount!
  • Compounding Frequency: How often interest is calculated (annually=1, quarterly=4, monthly=12, daily=365). More frequent compounding = more interest earned!
  • Time Value of Money: Money today is worth more than the same amount in the future because it can earn interest. This is why investing early is so powerful!
  • Power of Compounding: The longer you invest, the more dramatic the growth becomes. This is why compound interest is called "the eighth wonder of the world"!

How Compound Interest Works: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Initial Investment

You invest ₱10,000 at 5% annual interest, compounded annually.

Step 2: First Year

After 1 year: Interest = ₱10,000 × 0.05 = ₱500

New Balance = ₱10,000 + ₱500 = ₱10,500

Step 3: Second Year

Now interest is calculated on ₱10,500 (not just ₱10,000)!

Interest = ₱10,500 × 0.05 = ₱525

New Balance = ₱10,500 + ₱525 = ₱11,025

💡 Notice: You earned ₱25 more in year 2 than year 1!

Step 4: Third Year

Interest = ₱11,025 × 0.05 = ₱551.25

Final Balance = ₱11,025 + ₱551.25 = ₱11,576.25

💡 Total Interest Earned: ₱1,576.25 (vs. ₱1,500 with simple interest)

Example 1: Basic Compound Interest (Annual Compounding)

Problem:

Maria invests ₱10,000 at 5% annual interest, compounded annually for 3 years. Calculate the final amount and total interest earned.

Step-by-Step Solution:

Given: P = ₱10,000
r = 5% per year
n = 1 (compounded annually)
t = 3 years
Step 1: Convert rate to decimal:
r = 5% = 5 ÷ 100 = 0.05
Step 2: Calculate r/n:
r/n = 0.05 ÷ 1 = 0.05
Step 3: Calculate nt:
nt = 1 × 3 = 3
Step 4: Calculate (1 + r/n):
1 + r/n = 1 + 0.05 = 1.05
Step 5: Raise to power nt:
(1.05)^3 = 1.05 × 1.05 × 1.05 = 1.157625
Step 6: Calculate final amount:
A = P × (1.05)^3
A = 10,000 × 1.157625
A = ₱11,576.25
Step 7: Calculate total interest:
Interest = A - P
Interest = 11,576.25 - 10,000 = ₱1,576.25

Answer: After 3 years, Maria will have ₱11,576.25 and will have earned ₱1,576.25 in interest.

💡 Compare this to simple interest: I = 10,000 × 0.05 × 3 = ₱1,500. Compound interest earned ₱76.25 more!

2

Example 2: Quarterly Compounding

Problem:

John deposits ₱20,000 in a savings account that pays 6% annual interest, compounded quarterly. How much will he have after 2 years?

Step-by-Step Solution:

Given: P = ₱20,000
r = 6% per year
n = 4 (compounded quarterly)
t = 2 years
Step 1: Convert rate to decimal:
r = 6% = 0.06
Step 2: Calculate r/n:
r/n = 0.06 ÷ 4 = 0.015 (1.5% per quarter)
Step 3: Calculate nt:
nt = 4 × 2 = 8 (8 compounding periods)
Step 4: Calculate (1 + r/n):
1 + r/n = 1 + 0.015 = 1.015
Step 5: Raise to power nt:
(1.015)^8 = 1.126493 (approximately)
Step 6: Calculate final amount:
A = 20,000 × 1.126493
A = ₱22,529.86

Answer: After 2 years with quarterly compounding, John will have ₱22,529.86.

💡 With annual compounding: A = 20,000(1.06)^2 = ₱22,472. Quarterly compounding earned ₱57.86 more!

3

Example 3: Monthly Compounding

Problem:

Sarah invests ₱15,000 at 8% annual interest, compounded monthly for 18 months. Find the final amount.

Step-by-Step Solution:

Given: P = ₱15,000
r = 8% per year
n = 12 (compounded monthly)
t = 18 months = 1.5 years
Step 1: Convert rate to decimal:
r = 8% = 0.08
Step 2: Calculate r/n:
r/n = 0.08 ÷ 12 = 0.006667 (approximately 0.67% per month)
Step 3: Calculate nt:
nt = 12 × 1.5 = 18 (18 compounding periods)
Step 4: Calculate (1 + r/n):
1 + r/n = 1 + 0.006667 = 1.006667
Step 5: Raise to power nt:
(1.006667)^18 = 1.126825 (approximately)
Step 6: Calculate final amount:
A = 15,000 × 1.126825
A = ₱16,902.38

Answer: After 18 months with monthly compounding, Sarah will have ₱16,902.38.

💡 Monthly compounding gives you interest every month, which then earns more interest!

Understanding the Formula

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

A (Final Amount)

The total amount after interest is applied

Principal + Compound Interest

P (Principal)

The initial amount of money invested or borrowed

Starting amount

r (Annual Rate)

The annual interest rate as a decimal

5% = 0.05

n (Compounding Frequency)

How many times per year interest is compounded

Annually=1, Quarterly=4, Monthly=12

Formula Breakdown

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

  • A: Final amount (principal + interest)
  • P: Principal (initial amount)
  • r: Annual interest rate (as decimal)
  • n: Compounding frequency per year
  • t: Time in years
Example

P = 10,000, r = 6%, n = 4 (quarterly), t = 2 years

r/n = 0.06/4 = 0.015
nt = 4 × 2 = 8
A = 10000(1 + 0.015)^8
A = 10000(1.015)^8 = 11,264.93

Time Value of Money

The concept that money available today is worth more than the same amount in the future due to its potential earning capacity. This principle is fundamental to understanding compound interest.

Future Value (FV)

The value of money at a future date, given a specific interest rate and compounding frequency.

FV = PV(1 + r/n)^(nt)

Example:

What will ₱5,000 be worth in 3 years at 8% compounded quarterly?

PV = ₱5,000, r = 0.08, n = 4, t = 3
FV = 5000(1 + 0.08/4)^(4×3)
FV = 5000(1.02)^12 = ₱6,345.20

Present Value (PV)

The current value of money to be received in the future, discounted at a specific interest rate.

PV = FV / (1 + r/n)^(nt)

Example:

What is the present value of ₱10,000 to be received in 2 years at 6% compounded monthly?

FV = ₱10,000, r = 0.06, n = 12, t = 2
PV = 10000 / (1 + 0.06/12)^(12×2)
PV = 10000 / (1.005)^24 = ₱8,884.35
