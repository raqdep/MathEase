Lesson 2: Compound Interest Formula

Master the compound interest formula A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt) and its applications.

DepEd MELCs 2025-2026 Alignment

GM11-IIa-2: Solves problems involving compound interest

Formula Breakdown

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

  • A: Final amount (principal + interest)
  • P: Principal (initial amount)
  • r: Annual interest rate (as decimal)
  • n: Compounding frequency per year
  • t: Time in years
Example

P = 10,000, r = 6%, n = 4 (quarterly), t = 2 years

r/n = 0.06/4 = 0.015
nt = 4 × 2 = 8
A = 10000(1 + 0.015)^8
A = 10000(1.015)^8 = 11,264.93

Lesson 4: Present Value and Future Value

Learn about the time value of money and how to calculate present and future values.

Future Value

The value of money at a future date, given a specific interest rate.

FV = PV(1 + r/n)^(nt)

Present Value

The current value of money to be received in the future.

PV = FV / (1 + r/n)^(nt)
